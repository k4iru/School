Roasted Pork Belly (Siu Yuk)

Posted: October 6, 2020

The perfect recipe for crispy Cantonese style roasted pork belly.

Growing up, dinner time was always special at my home. My parents worked long hours. And I was always somewhere else getting into trouble. But despite our busy schedules, my parents made it a point to always eat dinner together. They specialized in traditional Cantonese cuisine and I have fond memories of many dishes, but Siu Yuk was by far my favourite.

The most important element to making great Siu Yuk is a nice crispy skin. The key is to pierce the pork belly skin with as many small holes as possible. The first thing we want to do is blanche the pork belly. Submerge the pork belly in boiling water for 10-15 minutes then remove and place it skin side up on a cutting board. Next, take a small fork and poke holes all over the skin. You want to be sure to pierce as deep as possible without going through the otherside of the skin. The more holes you make the better your dish will turn out. Piercing the skin helps release the oils and moisture allowing it to crisp up very nicely.

Once you're done pricking the skin, take a paper towel and wipe off any excess water that built up. Generously sprinkle salt on the skin and rub it in. After 30 minutes wipe any liquid that the salt draws out and place the pork belly skin side up in a shallow container of marinade. The marinade should come up the sides of the container covering the meat of the pork belly, but not touching the skin. If you have trouble finding an appropriate container you can make one out of aluminum foil, we will be needing one later anyways. Leave the container in the fridge overnight uncovered. This will help the skin dry even further while letting the pork belly soak up all that delicious marinade.

All thats left now is put it in the oven. If you haven't yet, take a large piece of aluminum foil and fold up the sides around the pork belly snugly, almost like you're making a box. This helps protect the meat from the high heat. Preheat the oven and brush the skin with some rice wine vinegar. Then place the pork belly in the center of the oven and roast for 50 minutes. Take the pork belly out of the oven and turn the broiler to low. Put the pork belly into the lowest tray as far from the heat source as possible. After 10-15 minutes remove when the skin has puffed nice and crispy.

Ingredients

  • 1.4 to 1.8 kgs skin-on pork belly
  • 1-2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 to 2 teaspoons rice wine vinegar

Marinade

  • 1/2 cup light soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup oyster sauce
  • 1/2 cup rice wine
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon fennel seeds

Instructions

  1. Blanche pork belly for 10-15 minutes and pat dry
  2. Pierce plenty of holes into the skin using a small fork
  3. Wipe dry with paper towel
  4. Rub a generous amount of salt into the skin then wipe away excess salt and liquid after letting sit for 30 minutes
  5. Mix together marinade and place pork belly skin side up into mixture
  6. Let pork belly sit in fridge uncovered overnight
  7. Preheat oven to 180C and brush pork belly skin with rice win vinegar
  8. Place pork belly in folded aluminum foil container into the oven for 50 minutes to an hour
  9. turn the broiler to low and continue cooking until skin puffs up
  10. Transfer to a cutting board and let cool for 5-10 minutes before cutting
